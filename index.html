About the Indaba

The 2026 Built Environment Indaba is a high-level platform uniting government, the private sector, academia, civil society and labour to unlock the full potential of South Africa's built environment. Through collaborative funding, skills development and innovative delivery models, the Indaba drives practical solutions to critical infrastructure challenges. By fostering meaningful partnerships across key sectors, the Indaba accelerates infrastructure delivery, economic transformation and long-term sustainability.

Overview

The Inaugural BBCBE Built Environment Indaba 2024, hosted by the Black Business Council in the Built Environment (BBCBE), was a pivotal event addressing South Africa's built environment sector. The BBCBE, a non-racial and non-political organisation, focuses on uniting black contractors, manufacturers, suppliers, and professionals to rectify historical inequalities and boost the participation of black businesses in the mainstream economy.

In 2025, the Indaba deepened this engagement by strengthening partnerships, refining sector-specific discussions, and aligning stakeholders around innovative funding, procurement reform, and skills development. Building on this momentum, the 2026 Built Environment Indaba represents a decisive shift from dialogue to delivery, focusing on coordinated action, mindset change, and practical implementation.

The 2026 Indaba prioritises collaborative funding models, accelerated skills development, and improved infrastructure delivery across key sectors including energy, water, transport and logistics, human settlements, and infrastructure finance, with the explicit aim of unlocking the built environment sector, driving inclusive economic growth, and ensuring long-term sustainability and resilience.
